{"text": "Probe into HIV women\u2019s\nsterilisation claim starts\n\nBY NATION REPORTER\nThe office of the Director of Public\nProsecutions has started investigations\ninto cases of women living with\nHIV and Aids who claim they were\nsterilised without their knowledge.\nThe DPP, Mr Keriako Tobiko, on\nWednesday appointed a task force\nto inquire into the matter and report\nback to him.\nThe seven member team, drawn\nfrom Mr Tobiko\u2019s office, will be led\nby senior state counsel Catherine\nMwaniki.\nThe public prosecutor said that the\nteam will work with an organisation,\nAfrican Gender and Media Initiative,\nwhich exposed the allegations by the\naffected women.\nThe task force and the organisation\nwill interview victims and\ncollect relevant evidence which can\nhelp prosecute those involved in the\nsterilisation.\n\u201cThe findings will enable me make\na decision whether there is evidence\nto sustain criminal charges against\nany person or group of people responsible\nfor the sterilisation,\u201d Mr\nTobiko told the Nation.\nThe prosecutor has also written\nto the executive director of the\ngender initiative, Ms Faith Kisava,\nrequesting her to submit to the task\nforce any relevant information and\nmaterial that may aid his office in\nthe investigations.\nThe study by the gender initiative\nreleased a report recently on the\ncases of 40 women who claim to\nhave been sterilised without their\nconsent.\nThe report titled, Robbed of\nChoice: Forced and Coerced Sterilisation\nof women with HIV in Kenya,\nclaims health workers were forcing\nwomen living with HIV to undergo\ncontraceptive sterilisation.\nThe report captures grim experiences\nthe women went through when\nthey went to give birth but instead\nwent home with their reproductive\norgans tampered with by the same\ndoctors who helped them deliver.\nThe affected women are from\nNairobi, Kakamega, Meru, Kandara,\nTigania and Kisumu."}
