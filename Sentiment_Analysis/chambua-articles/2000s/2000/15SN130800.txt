                                 Mr Sogomo said the commission would "up-
                             hold what is in the best interest of the country
                              and the teaching fraternity".
                                 Mr Ng'ang'a said some of the branches had
                              mandated the headquarters to handle the mat-
                              ter. He appealed to those that had not for-
                              warded appeals to do so immediately. Appeals
                              that have been received include those from
                              Kisii, Meru, Embu, Nyeri, Kirinyaga and
                              Thika.
                                 He said the branches that had given the com-
                              mission a IO-day ultimatum to revoke the
                              transfers had acted unprocedurally. "We are
                              talking about employment, hard times and
                              retrenchment and we need to do so soberly."
                                 But Mr Ng'ang'a said some sickly, newly
                              married, crippled, aged and expectant mothers
                              about to deliver had been transferred. "How
                              does one transfer a woman who will deliver in
                              the next three weeks? We shall defend our dig-
                             nity to the end."
                                 Education Assistant Minister Adams Karauri
                              said the issue was being handled soberly. How-
                              ever, Mr Karauri said the move was meant to
                              boost disadvantaged schools. The government
                              would not just sit back and watch when some
                              regions were overstaffed while others had
                              shortages.
