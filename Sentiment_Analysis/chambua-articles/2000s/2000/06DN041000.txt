       Internecine wars at the council are no longer news. Indeed, a
   good number of its sessions have ended up in fisticuffs. But the
   latest claims are unsettling for the simple reason that City Hall is
   in dire need of reorganisation if it is to deliver effective services
   to residents.
       The council has been in the doldrums for longer than most
   Nairobi residents care to remember. City infrastructure, including
   schools and hospitals, are in a deplorable state of disrepair. The
   council has more staff than it knows what to do with. Garbage
   collection was such a nightmare that residents came up with their
   own alternatives. Plot scandals, involving officers and council-
   lors, have been too numerous to count. The litany of ills is long.
