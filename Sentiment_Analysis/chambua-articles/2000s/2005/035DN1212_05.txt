Daily Nation / Monday, December 12, 2005

Kosgei still Nyeri race champion
By KAMORE MAINA
Nathaniel Kosgei, yesterday defended his
21 kilometre Safaricom Nyeri half marathon
title after his competitor, Francis
Maina was disqualified at the Dedan
Kimathi Stadium.
Maina was disqualified from the event after it
emerged that he had used a shortcut enroute to finishing
the race at the former Kamukunji grounds.
Kosgei timed 65:00:83, compared to the 1:05:03
he clocked last year.
“This year’s race was more challenging,” Kosgei
said.
He was closely followed by William Tadoo and
Philip Kiptoo who clocked 65:09:17 and 66:00:14
respectively.
In the women’s category Jane Nyambura finished
first ahead of Anastasia Ndereba, a sister of race
co-sponsor Catherine Ndereba the world renowned
marathoner.
Nyambura timed 77:56:35 with Ndereba clocking
78:25:49.
In the 10 kilometre race for men, Philip Kimtoi
was first while Consolata Chemtai took the lead in
the women’s category.
Kimtoi finished in a time of 30:43:55 with
Chemtai clocking 36:25.
Elizabeth Otieno and Gabriel Ouma finished
first in the women’s and men’s wheelchair category.
In the under 13 kilometre race for children, Peter
Gichuki and Beatrice Wanja were first in the boys
and girls category.
A total of 427 athletes participated in this ninth
edition of the event.
The race has helped tap and nurture several talented
runners ncluding the now legendary Catherine
Ndereba. Former 10,000m worldchampion
Charles Kamathi was also discovered in this race.
The event is aimed at sensitising the youths on
the hazards of drugs abuse HIV and Aids pandemic
according to race director Patrick Mwaura.
This years theme was Chase Aids Out of Kenya.
Vice President Moody Awori flagged off the
race.
