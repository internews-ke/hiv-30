{"text": "Daily Nation/ Wednesday, May 11, 2005\r\n\r\nADRA Officially Launches HIV and AIDS Project in Kenya and Tanzania\r\nNAIROBI, KENYA: The Adventist Development and Relief Agency (ADRA) is officially\r\nlaunching an Abstinence and Behaviour Change for Youth (ABY) project on May 11th, 2005,\r\nin Kisumu, Kenya, starting at 9am at Jomo Kenyatta Sports Ground. The launch is open to the\r\ngeneral public.\r\nThrough a five year grant of US$9,988,960 from the American people awarded by the United\r\nStates Development Agency (USAID), the project will be implemented through a consortium of\r\nHIV and AIDS prevention strategy from October 2004 to September 2009 in Kenya and\r\nTanzania, mainly in the Lake Victoria region.\r\nThe ABY project will expand skills-based HIV training for youth; facilitate positive community\r\ndiscourse on HIV/AIDS; and reinforce the role of parents and other protective influences.\r\nProject activities include reaching one million youth and adults with messages on abstinence,\r\nfidelity, and partner reduction, as well as avoidance of harmful behaviours such as coercive\r\nand transactional sex. An additional five million youth and adults will receive HIV and AIDS\r\nprevention education through the public media at national level.\r\nKey partners include government ministries of education, health and gender, sports, culture\r\nand social services (Kenya) and Labour and Youth (Tanzania), East-Central Division of the\r\nSeventh-day Adventist Church (East African and Tanzania Unions) and other faith based\r\norganizations within the project\ufffds areas of operation. The project will operate within the policy\r\nguidelines provided by the National AIDS Control Council (NACC) and Tanzania Commission\r\nfor AIDS (TACAIDS).\r\nADRA is a non-governmental organization (NGO) with a presence in more than 125 countries\r\nworldwide. ADRA\ufffds purpose is to develop human capacity, increase self-reliance, and\r\nempower communities to meet crisis or chronic distress. ADRA works to achieve positive\r\nchange without regard to ethnicity or religious affiliation.
